# 07. Glossary

Terminology used throughout the NIRE Assessment Framework.

---

## Framework terms

**Assessment**: The full set of answers a founder provides, scored and reported. One assessment per major framework version per founder.

**Dimension**: One of the 8 thematic areas the assessment is structured around (e.g. "Founder & Team", "Market"). Each dimension answers a specific VC underwriting question.

**Tier**: Within each dimension, the grouping of questions by depth. Required, recommended, optional.

**Archetype**: A defined VC investor profile (e.g. "Seed Generalist") that scores founder answers through its specific lens.

**Routing**: The logic that determines which questions a founder sees based on stage, business model, and region.

**Benchmark**: A reference value (e.g. "median seed round size in the UK") used to translate raw answers into scores.

**Recommendation template**: A structured guidance pattern triggered by specific scoring conditions, personalised at runtime using the founder's data.

**Confidence rating**: A meta-score (low / medium / high / very high) indicating how complete and reliable the founder's data is.

---

## Stage terminology

**Pre-seed**: First institutional or angel money, typically £100k-£750k in the UK, often pre-revenue or with very early traction. Usually team and idea bet.

**Seed**: Typically £500k-£3m in the UK, post-MVP, with early evidence of product-market fit. Often the first priced round.

**Series A**: Typically £3m-£15m in the UK, growth-stage with proven product-market fit and unit economics signal. Used to scale a working model.

**Bridge / Extension**: An interim round between defined stages, often raised on convertible instruments.

---

## Investor terminology

**VC**: Venture capital firm.

**LP (Limited Partner)**: An investor in a VC fund (e.g. pension fund, family office, sovereign wealth).

**GP (General Partner)**: A partner at a VC firm, responsible for investment decisions and fund management.

**IC (Investment Committee)**: The formal decision-making body within a VC fund.

**Cheque size**: The amount a single investor commits in a round.

**Lead investor**: The investor who sets the terms of the round and typically takes the largest cheque.

**Follow-on**: Subsequent investment by an existing investor in later rounds.

---

## UK-specific terminology

**SEIS (Seed Enterprise Investment Scheme)**: UK tax relief for investors in very early-stage companies. Investors get 50% income tax relief. Highly attractive for UK angels.

**EIS (Enterprise Investment Scheme)**: UK tax relief for investors in early-stage companies. Investors get 30% income tax relief.

**EMI (Enterprise Management Incentive)**: UK tax-advantaged share option scheme for employees of qualifying companies.

**Advance Assurance**: HMRC pre-approval that a company qualifies for SEIS/EIS. Often expected by UK angels before investing.

**Innovate UK**: UK government innovation agency offering grants and support.

**R&D Tax Credits**: UK tax relief for qualifying research and development expenditure.

---

## France-specific terminology

**BSPCE (Bons de Souscription de Parts de Créateur d'Entreprise)**: French tax-advantaged share warrants for employees of qualifying companies. Roughly equivalent to EMI options.

**JEI (Jeune Entreprise Innovante)**: French status for young innovative companies, providing tax and social charge relief.

**BPI France**: French public investment bank providing financing and support to startups.

**CIR (Crédit d'Impôt Recherche)**: French research tax credit.

---

## Germany-specific terminology

**GmbH (Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung)**: Standard German limited liability company structure.

**UG (Unternehmergesellschaft)**: "Mini-GmbH", a German LLC with lower capital requirements.

**AG (Aktiengesellschaft)**: German public limited company.

**VSOP (Virtual Stock Option Plan)**: Common German alternative to traditional options, due to historic tax complications around real options for employees.

**EXIST**: German government startup grant programme.

**KfW**: German state-owned development bank providing startup financing.

---

## Metrics and unit economics

**MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)**: Subscription revenue earned in a given month.

**ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)**: MRR × 12, or the annual run-rate of subscription revenue.

**MoM Growth**: Month-on-month growth rate.

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**: Total sales and marketing spend divided by new customers acquired.

**LTV (Lifetime Value)**: Expected total revenue from a customer over their relationship with the company.

**LTV:CAC ratio**: A common health metric. Generally, 3:1 or higher is considered healthy.

**CAC Payback**: Time taken to recover CAC from gross profit. Shorter is better.

**Gross Margin**: Revenue minus cost of goods sold, as a percentage of revenue.

**Burn Rate**: Net cash outflow per month.

**Runway**: Cash on hand divided by burn rate, expressed in months.

**Churn**: Percentage of customers (or revenue) lost over a defined period.

**Net Revenue Retention**: Recurring revenue from existing customers, including upgrades, downgrades, and churn. Above 100% is exceptional.

---

## Marketplace terminology

**Two-sided marketplace**: A platform with two distinct user types whose value derives from each other (here: founders and VCs).

**Cold start problem**: The challenge of building initial value on both sides simultaneously.

**Chicken-and-egg**: Synonymous with cold start.

**Liquidity**: The probability that a user on one side finds value on the other (here: a VC finding a relevant founder, a founder finding interest).

---

## Product terminology used in this repo

**Required tier**: The minimum questions needed for a meaningful score.

**Recommended tier**: Additional questions that improve confidence and sharpness.

**Optional deep-dive**: Questions for founders who want maximum precision.

**Adaptive routing**: The logic that hides irrelevant questions based on founder context.

**Gap analysis**: The specific differences between a founder's current state and the threshold for the next score band.

**Underwriting question**: The fundamental question a VC needs to answer to make an investment decision.
