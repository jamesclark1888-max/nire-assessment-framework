# 00. Overview

This document is the guided tour of the NIRE Assessment Framework. If you are new to the repository, read this first.

---

## What NIRE is solving

VC fund managers struggle to filter, compare, and underwrite early-stage founders consistently. The signal-to-noise ratio in inbound deal flow is poor, data is unstandardised, and assessment is often vibes-driven.

Founders, on their side, want to raise but don't know what specifically is holding them back. Generic "investment readiness" tools tell them to "improve your pitch deck" without explaining what a VC actually looks for.

NIRE sits between the two: a rigorous founder assessment that produces VC-grade data on one side, and an actionable improvement roadmap for founders on the other.

---

## How the framework works at a glance

A founder lands on NIRE and answers a structured questionnaire. The questionnaire:

1. **Adapts** to their stage, business model, and region
2. **Scores** their answers across 8 dimensions
3. **Applies multiple VC archetype lenses** to show how different investor types would view them
4. **Surfaces gaps** with specific, actionable recommendations
5. **Generates a partial report** for free, with a paid upgrade for the full report
6. **Feeds standardised data** to the VC side of the marketplace (gated by VC plan tier)

---

## The 8 dimensions

Every assessment is structured around 8 dimensions, each answering a specific VC underwriting question:

| # | Dimension | The VC question it answers |
|---|-----------|----------------------------|
| 1 | Founder & Team | Are these the right people, and can they attract more? |
| 2 | Problem & Insight | What non-obvious truth does this team know? |
| 3 | Market | Is the opportunity big enough? |
| 4 | Product & Traction | Is it working? |
| 5 | Business Model & Unit Economics | Can this make money? |
| 6 | Go-to-Market | Can they reach customers? |
| 7 | Competition & Moat | Why won't this get crushed? |
| 8 | Round & Capital Strategy | Is this fundable now? |

---

## Question tiers

Within each dimension, questions are grouped into three tiers:

- **Required** (3-5 per dimension): minimum needed to produce a score. ~25-40 questions total across all dimensions.
- **Recommended** (5-8 per dimension): improve scoring confidence and sharpen recommendations.
- **Optional deep-dive**: for founders who want maximum precision.

A founder who completes only the required tier gets a meaningful score with a "medium confidence" rating. Completing more questions increases confidence and unlocks sharper recommendations.

---

## Regional adaptation

The framework is launching with UK as the deepest-supported region, with strong support for major EU markets and lighter support elsewhere. See [`03-regional-strategy.md`](03-regional-strategy.md) for the full approach.

Geographic adaptation affects:

- Which questions are asked (e.g. EMI/EIS for UK, BSPCE/JEI for France)
- What benchmarks are applied (UK pre-seed rounds differ in size from US rounds)
- Which terminology is used
- Which VC archetypes are most weighted

---

## VC archetypes

Same answers are scored through multiple lenses simultaneously:

- Seed Generalist
- Sector Specialist
- Growth / Series A
- Angel Syndicate
- Strategic / Corporate VC

A founder sees their overall NIRE score plus a breakdown of how each archetype would view them. This is one of the core differentiators.

See [`04-vc-archetypes.md`](04-vc-archetypes.md).

---

## The free vs paid split

A founder gets a free partial report. Paying unlocks:

- The full set of recommendations
- The detailed gap analysis per dimension
- A downloadable PDF report
- The ability to be discoverable on the VC side of the marketplace

VC-side consumption is also tiered: basic plans see anonymised aggregates, premium plans see full founder profiles with consent.

See [`06-paywall-strategy.md`](06-paywall-strategy.md).
