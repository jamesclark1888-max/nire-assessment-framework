# Example founder journey: UK pre-seed SaaS

This worked example illustrates how the NIRE framework processes a representative founder profile. It is illustrative only; no real founder data is used.

---

## Founder profile

**Name (pseudonymous):** Sarah Chen
**Company:** Verity Operations Ltd
**Stage:** Pre-seed (raising first round)
**Business model:** B2B SaaS
**Region:** United Kingdom (incorporated in England)
**Sector:** Operations software for mid-market manufacturing

---

## What Sarah sees

When Sarah signs up, she selects:

- Country of incorporation: United Kingdom
- Primary market: UK + Western Europe
- Founder location: London
- Stage: Pre-seed
- Business model: B2B SaaS

The routing engine builds her question set: approximately 36 questions across the 8 dimensions, including 2 UK-specific questions.

## Sarah's answers (selected)

### Dimension 1: Founder & Team

**q1.1 (co-founders):** 2
**q1.2 (co-founder details):**
- Sarah Chen (CEO): 12 years in mid-market manufacturing operations, ex-McKinsey then VP Ops at a £200m revenue manufacturer. 50% equity, full-time.
- Marcus Patel (CTO): 9 years in SaaS engineering, previously Tech Lead at a UK fintech that exited. 50% equity, full-time.

**q1.3 (working relationship):** 1-3 years (they collaborated as consultant + client for 18 months before co-founding)

**q1.4 (function coverage):** Technical (Marcus), Product (Marcus), Domain expertise (Sarah). Missing: commercial, marketing.

**q1.5 (founder-market fit):**
*"I spent 12 years inside mid-market manufacturing, the last 6 leading operations transformations at a £200m revenue UK manufacturer. I personally lived the problem we're now solving: every operations transformation gets stuck on the same data fragmentation issue, and existing software solutions are built for enterprise (SAP, Oracle) or for very small businesses (Xero, QuickBooks-adjacent tools). Nothing serves mid-market. Marcus built and shipped three internal tools at his previous fintech that solved analogous data integration problems. Together we have the domain depth and the technical depth to do this credibly. I've already had 47 customer conversations with operations directors at UK manufacturers and 18 of them have committed to design partnerships if we ship a v1 in the next 6 months."*

**q1.uk1 (EMI):** Planning to set up
**q1.uk2 (SEIS/EIS):** Applied, awaiting response

### Other dimensions (summarised for brevity)

- Strong qualitative founder-market fit
- 47 customer interviews completed, with documented learnings
- Realistic TAM build-up using bottom-up methodology
- Pre-revenue but 18 LOIs from design partners
- Clear unit economics theory; not yet measured
- Founder-led sales strategy clear; explicit hiring plan for first commercial hire post-raise
- Three direct competitors identified with thoughtful differentiation
- Raising £500k at £4m pre-money, milestones explicitly linked

### Top recommendations (free tier)

1. **Critical: Address commercial leadership gap** (from q1.4)
   *Gap analysis: Sarah and Marcus cover technical, product, and domain expertise but there's no commercial leader on the team. At pre-seed this is acceptable but with a clear plan; without one it becomes a seed-round blocker.*
   *Fix: Name your first commercial hire publicly in your pitch deck. Set aside ~10% of round for this role. Begin candidate conversations now so a named candidate exists at investor meetings.*

2. **High: Complete SEIS/EIS Advance Assurance** (from q1.uk2)
   *Gap analysis: Application is in but not yet confirmed. UK angel investors prefer to see confirmation before committing.*
   *Fix: Follow up with HMRC. Have draft correspondence ready to share. Engage your accountant on chasing.*

3. **High: Implement founder vesting** (from q1.12 if answered, or as a structural recommendation)

## What this illustrates

- **Confidence ratings**: Sarah's data is dense, so most dimensions are Medium-High or High confidence
- **Archetype divergence**: Sector specialists view her substantially better than Growth/Series A would (she's not at that stage yet)
- **Free-tier value**: Even without paying, she gets useful signal on her most critical gaps
- **Action specificity**: Her top recommendation is not "improve your team" but "name your first commercial hire in your pitch deck"
- **Regional integration**: UK-specific guidance (SEIS/EIS, EMI) is included natively
- **The honesty principle**: A 76/100 is genuinely good but not perfect, and the report is specific about why
