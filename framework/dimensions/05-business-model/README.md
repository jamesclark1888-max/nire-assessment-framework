# Dimension 5: Business Model & Unit Economics

**The VC underwriting question this answers:** *"Can this make money? Do the economics work at scale?"*

**Status:** 🟡 Skeleton. Questions to be drafted in v0.2.0.

---

## Why this dimension exists

A growing user base with unworkable unit economics is the most expensive kind of failure. This dimension assesses whether the path from customer to profit is plausible.

At pre-seed, this is often theoretical. At seed, it should be modelled. At Series A, it should be evidenced.

---

## What this dimension assesses (planned)

1. **Revenue model**: how money is made (subscription, transaction, marketplace, one-time, etc.)
2. **Pricing**: explicit pricing and rationale
3. **CAC**: cost to acquire a customer
4. **LTV**: expected lifetime value
5. **Gross margins**: structural unit economics
6. **Payback period**: time to recover CAC

---

## Planned question structure

### Required tier (estimated 5-6 questions)
- Primary revenue model
- Current pricing structure
- Estimated or measured CAC
- Estimated or measured LTV
- Gross margin (or path to it)
- Payback period

### Recommended tier (estimated 6-7 questions)
- CAC breakdown by channel
- LTV calculation methodology
- Pricing power evidence (price changes, willingness to pay)
- Unit economics sensitivity analysis
- Cost structure breakdown
- Expansion revenue mechanics

### Optional deep-dive (estimated 4 questions)
- Financial model upload
- Detailed cohort economics
- Comparable company benchmarking
- Sensitivity scenarios

---

## Business-model adaptation

This dimension's questions vary heavily by business model:

- **SaaS**: MRR/ARR, gross margin (target >75%), NRR (target >100%)
- **Marketplace**: take rate, GMV, transaction frequency
- **Hardware**: gross margin (often 30-50%), inventory dynamics
- **DTC**: AOV, repeat rate, contribution margin
- **Deeptech**: more emphasis on long-term unit economics path
- **Services**: less venture-typical; unit economics specifically interrogated
