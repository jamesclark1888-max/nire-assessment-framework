# Dimension 3: Market

**The VC underwriting question this answers:** *"Is the opportunity big enough to be venture-scale?"*

**Status:** 🟡 Skeleton. Questions to be drafted in v0.2.0.

---

## Why this dimension exists

VCs need outsized outcomes. A small but profitable business is not a venture business. The market dimension assesses whether the opportunity is large enough, growing enough, and reachable enough to justify venture risk.

This dimension is also one of the most commonly fudged. Founders inflate TAM through dubious methodologies. The framework specifically rewards rigorous bottom-up reasoning.

---

## What this dimension assesses (planned)

1. **TAM / SAM / SOM rigour**: how the founder calculates market size
2. **Market growth dynamics**: is the market growing or static
3. **Customer segment definition**: who exactly is the customer
4. **Reachability**: can the founder actually reach this market
5. **Timing**: why this market is hot or becoming hot

---

## Planned question structure

### Required tier (estimated 4-5 questions)
- TAM with stated methodology
- SAM (addressable subset) with stated methodology
- SOM (realistic 5-year share) with stated methodology
- Market growth rate
- Primary customer segment definition

### Recommended tier (estimated 5-6 questions)
- Customer segment breakdown (firmographics or demographics)
- Comparable companies and their valuations
- Market timing thesis
- Adjacent market expansion opportunities
- Market structure (fragmented vs consolidated)

### Optional deep-dive (estimated 3-4 questions)
- Sources and methodology documentation
- Forward-looking market analysis
- International expansion sequencing
- Market structure analysis

---

## Cross-region sensitivity

Market sizing for UK/EU-focused companies is intrinsically smaller than US-focused. Regional benchmarks adjust thresholds accordingly. A "small TAM" in absolute terms may be large relative to the addressable customer base.
