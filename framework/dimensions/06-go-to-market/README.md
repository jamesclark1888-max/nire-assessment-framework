# Dimension 6: Go-to-Market

**The VC underwriting question this answers:** *"Can they reach customers efficiently and repeatably?"*

**Status:** 🟡 Skeleton. Questions to be drafted in v0.2.0.

---

## Why this dimension exists

A great product without distribution is a hobby. VCs increasingly weight GTM capability heavily because product-market fit is often achievable but GTM scale is harder.

This dimension assesses the founder's GTM strategy, evidence of working channels, and ability to scale acquisition.

---

## What this dimension assesses (planned)

1. **GTM strategy clarity**: do they have one
2. **Working channels**: which acquisition channels are producing customers today
3. **Repeatability**: can they reliably produce more customers
4. **Founder-led sales vs systematised**: stage-appropriate handoff
5. **Channel economics**: which channels are most efficient

---

## Planned question structure

### Required tier (estimated 4-5 questions)
- Primary GTM motion (PLG, founder-led sales, channel partnerships, etc.)
- Current customer acquisition channels (multi-select)
- Best-performing channel
- Sales cycle length
- Conversion rates (top of funnel to closed)

### Recommended tier (estimated 5-7 questions)
- Pipeline value
- Sales team composition (founder-only vs salespeople)
- Repeatability evidence (consistent monthly cohorts)
- Pricing and discounting dynamics
- Channel mix by revenue contribution
- Top customer acquisition stories

### Optional deep-dive (estimated 3-4 questions)
- CRM data screenshot uploads
- Detailed funnel breakdown
- Channel-by-channel economics
- 12-month GTM roadmap

---

## Stage adaptation

- **Pre-seed**: directional GTM thinking is enough
- **Seed**: evidence of one working channel
- **Series A**: evidence of repeatable, scalable acquisition
