# Dimension 2: Problem & Insight

**The VC underwriting question this answers:** *"What non-obvious truth does this team know about this problem?"*

**Status:** 🟡 Skeleton. Questions to be drafted in v0.2.0.

---

## Why this dimension exists

The best startups are built on an insight that's not yet widely understood. They see a problem clearly that most people see blurrily, or they see something true that the market hasn't yet priced in.

This dimension assesses the quality of the founder's insight, the depth of their problem understanding, and the customer evidence behind it.

---

## What this dimension assesses (planned)

1. **Problem articulation**: how clearly the founder describes the problem
2. **Customer evidence**: how many real customers they've spoken to and what they learned
3. **Insight depth**: what non-obvious truth they've identified
4. **Problem urgency**: why this needs solving now (timing thesis)
5. **Counter-intuitive elements**: what they believe that most people don't

---

## Planned question structure

### Required tier (estimated 4-5 questions)
- Problem statement in plain language
- Customer interviews completed (number + roles)
- Key insight or non-obvious truth
- Why now (timing thesis)
- Evidence the problem is real and urgent

### Recommended tier (estimated 5-6 questions)
- Specific customer quotes or stories
- Problem severity quantification
- Competitive alternatives customers currently use
- Failed prior attempts to solve this
- Counter-intuitive beliefs about the market

### Optional deep-dive (estimated 3-4 questions)
- Customer development log uploads
- Demographic/firmographic breakdown of customers interviewed
- Hypothesis evolution narrative
- Personal experience of the problem
