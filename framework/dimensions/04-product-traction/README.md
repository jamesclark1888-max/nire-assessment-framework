# Dimension 4: Product & Traction

**The VC underwriting question this answers:** *"Is it working? Is the product solving the problem and gaining adoption?"*

**Status:** 🟡 Skeleton. Questions to be drafted in v0.2.0.

---

## Why this dimension exists

At pre-seed, this dimension is light (often pre-product). At seed, it starts to matter materially. At Series A, it's the centre of gravity of the assessment.

The framework needs to handle:

- Pre-revenue companies (qualitative traction signals: pilots, LOIs, waitlists)
- Early revenue companies (MRR/ARR, growth, retention)
- Scaling companies (cohort retention, expansion, payback)

---

## What this dimension assesses (planned)

1. **Product status**: idea / prototype / MVP / live with users / scaling
2. **Quantitative traction**: revenue, users, growth, retention
3. **Qualitative traction**: pilots, LOIs, customer love signals
4. **Engagement and retention**: are users staying
5. **Product roadmap clarity**: what's next and why

---

## Planned question structure

### Required tier (estimated 5-6 questions)
- Current product status
- Active users / customers (count)
- MRR / ARR (if applicable)
- Month-over-month growth
- Retention metric (cohort or logo)
- Top customer or pilot evidence

### Recommended tier (estimated 6-8 questions)
- Cohort retention breakdown
- Net revenue retention (if SaaS)
- Engagement metric appropriate to model
- Top 5 customers detail
- Product roadmap (12-month view)
- Recent product launches and impact

### Optional deep-dive (estimated 4-5 questions)
- Stripe / payment processor screenshot uploads
- Cohort analysis upload
- Product analytics dashboard sharing
- Detailed roadmap doc upload

---

## Heavy stage adaptation

Question relevance varies dramatically:

- **Pre-seed**: focus on qualitative signals and pilot evidence
- **Seed**: balance of early metrics and qualitative
- **Series A**: heavy quantitative emphasis (cohort retention, NRR, expansion)
