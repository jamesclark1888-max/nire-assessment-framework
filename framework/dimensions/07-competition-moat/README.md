# Dimension 7: Competition & Moat

**The VC underwriting question this answers:** *"Why won't this get crushed by competitors or a copycat?"*

**Status:** 🟡 Skeleton. Questions to be drafted in v0.2.0.

---

## Why this dimension exists

Every startup has competition. Founders who claim "no direct competition" lose credibility instantly. The framework assesses how rigorously the founder thinks about the competitive landscape and what defensible advantages they're building.

Moats compound over time. The earlier a founder identifies their moat, the more deliberately they can build it.

---

## What this dimension assesses (planned)

1. **Competitive awareness**: do they know who their competitors are
2. **Differentiation**: what's specifically different about their approach
3. **Moat type**: network effects, technical, data, brand, switching costs, regulatory
4. **Moat strength evidence**: actual proof points
5. **Big-player risk**: what happens if Microsoft/Google/Amazon enters

---

## Planned question structure

### Required tier (estimated 4-5 questions)
- Top 3 direct competitors named
- What's specifically different about your approach
- Primary moat type
- Evidence the moat is real and growing
- Plausible big-player threat and response

### Recommended tier (estimated 5-6 questions)
- Competitive landscape map
- Win/loss analysis
- Switching cost mechanics
- IP and patent status
- Network effect quantification (if applicable)
- Data advantage development (if applicable)

### Optional deep-dive (estimated 3-4 questions)
- Detailed competitive battle cards
- IP documentation
- Customer reasons-to-stay data
- Strategic partnership moats
